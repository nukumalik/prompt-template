### Prompt:

```
Role:
You are an experienced SaaS founder and product strategist who has successfully built and scaled multiple "boring but profitable" B2B SaaS products to at least $10K MRR, primarily in markets like the US, Australia, and global remote businesses.

Context:
I am a software engineer who wants to build a web-based application as a source of income.

My goals:
- Build a "boring but profitable" product (high demand, recurring usage, proven willingness to pay)
- Keep the product extremely simple and fast to develop (MVP within [TIMEFRAME])
- Build it as a [TEAM_SIZE] (e.g., solo developer)
- Reach revenue as early as possible (within [TIME_TO_REVENUE])
- Focus on solving painful, specific problems (not “nice to have”)

Target market:
- Location: [TARGET_LOCATION]
- Audience: [TARGET_AUDIENCE] (be as specific as possible, e.g., “freelance accountants”, “small Shopify store owners”, etc.)

Technical preferences:
- Stack: [TECH_STACK]
- Complexity: LOW (avoid complex systems, AI-heavy solutions, or multi-sided platforms)

Task:
Generate [NUMBER_OF_IDEAS] highly practical SaaS product ideas that I can realistically build and launch as an MVP.

For each idea, provide:

1. Idea name (clear and descriptive, not generic)
2. Specific problem it solves (must be painful, frequent, and costly if unsolved)
3. Target user persona (very specific niche)
4. Real-world scenario (how the problem happens in daily workflow)
5. Why this is "boring but profitable"
   - Evidence of demand (existing behavior, tools people already use, manual workaround, etc.)
6. MVP scope (maximum 3–5 features, strictly essential only)
7. Monetization strategy
   - Suggested pricing (e.g., $9/mo, $29/mo)
   - Why users would realistically pay
8. Competitors
   - Mention real competitors or alternatives (including manual solutions like spreadsheets)
9. Market gap / opportunity
   - What existing tools do poorly
   - Your simple but strong differentiation
10. Go-to-market idea (VERY important)
   - Where to find users (specific platforms, communities, keywords)
   - First 10 paying users strategy

Constraints:
- DO NOT suggest:
  - Marketplaces
  - Social networks
  - Complex AI-first products
- Prioritize:
  - Tools that replace spreadsheets/manual work
  - Tools used weekly or daily
  - Clear ROI for users (save time, increase money, reduce risk)
- Ideas must be buildable using [TECH_STACK] within [TIMEFRAME]
- Ideas must be able to generate revenue within [TIME_TO_REVENUE]

Output format:
- Use clean Markdown
- Separate each idea clearly with headings (## Idea 1, ## Idea 2, etc.)
- Keep explanations concise but actionable
- Avoid generic advice — be specific and realistic
```
